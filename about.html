## About Narayanan Rengaswamy was born and raised in the southern Indian coastal city named Chennai (fondly, Madras). He is currently pursuing his doctoral studies under the supervision of [Prof. Henry D. Pfister](http://pfister.ee.duke.edu) in the [information iniative at Duke (iiD)](http://bigdata.duke.edu) group of the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Duke University, Durham, NC, USA. Prior to this, he completed his Master of Science degree at Texas A&M University where he worked on the construction of Cyclic Polar Codes. He was a graduate research intern during the summer of 2015 at Alcatel-Lucent Bell Labs, Stuttgart, Germany, where he worked on the analysis of Spatially Coupled LDPC Codes over burst erasure channels, under the supervision of [Dr. Laurent Schmalen](https://www.bell-labs.com/usr/laurent.schmalen) and [Dr. Vahid Aref](https://www.bell-labs.com/usr/vahid.aref). His general interests are in the realms of information theory, statistics and their applications. Recently, he has started looking to problems in the fields of compressed sensing and machine learning, with specific interests in deep learning and its theory. He is a Student Member of the IEEE and the [Information Theory Society](http://itsoc.org). He loves pencil sketching and likes to read about science, philosophy and life. He plays cricket and volleyball. He is a novice writer and hopes to disseminate productive thought and knowledge through this powerful platform called internet.
