Updates

## Updates **Oct. 2018**: We have unified the [diagonal gates in the Clifford hierarchy](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1608.06596.pdf) with the symplectic representation of the Clifford group by extending binary symmetric matrices to rings of integers. Paper submitted to [QIP 2019](http://jila.colorado.edu/qip2019/) for a poster; full version in preparation. **Sep. 2018**: Using the automorphism group of classical Kerdock codes we have simplified the construction of the unitary 2-design by [Cleve et al. (2016)](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1501.04592.pdf). Code is available on [GitHub](https://github.com/nrenga/symplectic-arxiv18a).
Paper submitted to [QIP 2019](http://jila.colorado.edu/qip2019/); full version in preparation. **July 2018**: I participated in [TQC 2018](https://www.tqc2018.org/), and presented a poster on [logical Clifford synthesis](/files/posters/tqc2018_poster.pdf). **June 2018**: I presented our logical Clifford synthesis work in [ISIT 2018](/files/presentations/Rengaswamy-isit18.slides.pdf). **June 2018**: I mentored three Duke Math undergraduates, as part of the [Duke Opportunities in Mathematics (DOmath)](https://math.duke.edu/domath) program, on a quantum computing project lead by
[Prof. Robert Calderbank](https://math.duke.edu/people/robert-calderbank) and [Prof. Henry Pfister](http://pfister.ee.duke.edu). **May 2018**: I presented a [poster](/files/posters/symplectic_poster.pdf) on logical Clifford synthesis in [NASIT 2018](http://shannon.engr.tamu.edu). **Apr. 2018**: I gave a seminar on [Synthesis of Logical Operators for Quantum Computers using Stabilizer Codes](/files/presentations/rn_iitm_talk_2018.pdf) in the Department of Electrical Engineering, Indian Institute of Technology, Madras. **Mar. 2018**: Our paper on [Synthesis of Logical Clifford Operators via Symplectic Geometry](https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.06987) is posted on ArXiv. The source code for our algorithms is available on [GitHub](https://github.com/nrenga/symplectic-arxiv18a). Submitted to [Quantum](https://quantum-journal.org/). **Jan. 2018**: Our paper on the [Finite-Length Analysis of Spatially-Coupled Regular LDPC Ensembles on Burst-Erasure Channels](http://arxiv.org/abs/1611.08267) has been accepted for publication in the [IEEE Trans. on Inform. Theory](http://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/8272426/).
